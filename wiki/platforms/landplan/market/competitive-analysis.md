# LandPlan — Competitive Analysis

*Source: Internal research, April 2026.*

---

## Executive Summary

LandPlan operates at the intersection of GIS mapping and long-horizon property planning for individual landowners — a combination no existing product serves well. The competitive landscape divides into four buckets, none occupying LandPlan's target position:

- **Land id® / MapRight** — strong parcel/property data browser for real estate professionals; minimal planning, no owner-vision features
- **Aspire PropertyIntel** — precision measurement and takeoff for landscape contractors; $199–$499/mo B2B pricing
- **STACK CT** — construction estimating platform for contractors; no map-centric visualisation for owners
- **onX Hunt** — land navigation for hunters; strong public/private boundary data, zero planning capability

**The property owner who wants a 5-to-15-year vision for their raw land — with maps, 3D, projects, and contractor quoting — has no purpose-built tool today.**

---

## Competitor Profiles

### Land id® (formerly MapRight)

Leading property identity and interactive mapping platform, rebranded from MapRight in 2023.

**Strengths:** 150M+ parcel records; rich GIS layers (soil, floodplains, contours, ownership); mobile app with offline access; map sharing by embed/link/email; 3D terrain view.

**Gaps:** No project planning, sequencing, or activity tracking. No 3D model placement or shadow simulation. No stakeholder/contractor collaboration. No long-horizon vision. Data lives on Land id's servers.

**Audience:** Real estate brokers, appraisers, serious land buyers. "Discover" tier extends to individual landowners but product DNA is research and listing, not stewardship.

**Pricing:** $7/mo (Base) → $15/mo (Premium) → $50/mo (Pro) → $80/mo (Pro Unlimited).

---

### Aspire PropertyIntel

Measurement and estimating tool for landscape and irrigation contractors; integrated into Aspire CRM.

**Strengths:** Precision takeoff over aerial imagery; automated time/material estimating from contractor pricing databases; visual proposals with colour-coded sitemaps; AI-assisted slope visualisation.

**Gaps:** Not designed for property owners. No owner-facing planning, vision, or collaboration. Pricing ($199–$499+/mo) for businesses, not individuals.

**Audience:** Landscape and irrigation contractors. The landowner is the customer's customer.

---

### STACK CT

Cloud-based construction takeoff and estimating platform for general and specialty contractors.

**Strengths:** Digital plan/blueprint uploads with measurement layering; material quantity calculations; activity and bid management; contractor collaboration.

**Gaps:** No GIS mapping (works from uploaded PDFs). No property owner features. No 3D visualisation or long-term vision. $250/mo entry.

---

### onX Hunt

GPS navigation and land ownership mapping app for hunters.

**Strengths:** Public/private land boundary overlays across all 50 states; offline GPS; land ownership identification; very affordable ($30–$100/yr).

**Gaps:** No project planning or improvement sequencing. No photo documentation on map features. No 3D, no collaboration, no storage integration.

**Audience:** Hunters and outdoor recreationists. Small subset are landowners checking boundaries — nothing for what to build or how to sequence improvements.

**Note:** onX Hunt's success at $30–100/yr with millions of users demonstrates consumer willingness to pay for good mobile land tools. LandPlan's planned offline-first mobile app ([[survey-mobile]]) targets the same audience with far greater planning depth.

## Strategic Positioning

### The gap is structural

Tools that exist serve contractors (measure/estimate land for profit) or real estate professionals (research/sell land). Nobody builds for the person who *owns* the land and wants to steward it over decades. LandPlan's SaaS model, export capability, and planning depth give it a path pure-mapping apps don't have.

### Pricing has room

Cheapest mapping overlap: Land id® at ~$15/mo for basic tiers. LandPlan can position at $15–30/mo and deliver far more planning value, while being dramatically cheaper than contractor tools ($199–$499/mo).

### Closest risk: Land id® expanding upmarket

If Land id® added project planning and stakeholder collaboration, they could compete directly. Their current trajectory — real estate pro tooling, AI-assisted research — suggests their focus is elsewhere. Worth monitoring.

### Key moats

1. **Contractor quoting module** — no consumer land tool offers structured contractor quoting. LandPlan's Quote Packages differentiate from mapping tools *and* general PM tools like Notion.
2. **Data export and portability** — every competitor locks user data in their infrastructure. LandPlan's export capability (GCS + zip download + Google Drive) is a meaningful trust differentiator.
3. **Mobile + RTK path** — [[survey-mobile]] + [[survey-gps-receiver]] give LandPlan a hardware/mobile story no competitor is building.
