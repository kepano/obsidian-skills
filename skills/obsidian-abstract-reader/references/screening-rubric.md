# First-Pass Screening Rubric

Score PubMed abstracts on a 100-point scale. This is for initial triage, not a formal risk-of-bias, GRADE, or full-text appraisal.

## Score Components

| Component | Points | Guidance |
|---|---:|---|
| Journal level | 0-25 | Use `journal-tiers.md`; mark unknown tiers explicitly. |
| Recency | 0-15 | Score by publication year relative to the current year. |
| Method completeness | 0-25 | Reward clear design, population, sample size, methods, outcomes, and result details. |
| Scientific rigor | 0-20 | Reward clear question, design-conclusion fit, cautious claims, and appropriate statistics. |
| Novelty | 0-15 | Reward new evidence, population, method, model, mechanism, or clinically useful update. |

## Recency

| Age | Points |
|---|---:|
| 0-2 years | 15 |
| 3-5 years | 12 |
| 6-10 years | 8 |
| 11-15 years | 4 |
| More than 15 years | 1 |

If a paper is old but likely foundational, keep the age score but mention `older but potentially important`.

## Method Completeness

| Points | Description |
|---:|---|
| 21-25 | Design, population, sample size, methods, outcomes, and main results are clear. |
| 16-20 | Most method details are present; one or two key details are missing. |
| 10-15 | Direction is understandable but design, sample, outcome, or analysis details are weak. |
| 5-9 | Mostly conclusion-driven; methods are sparse. |
| 0-4 | No abstract or methods cannot be judged. |

## Scientific Rigor

| Points | Description |
|---:|---|
| 17-20 | Question is clear; design supports conclusion; claims are cautious and data-linked. |
| 13-16 | Generally sound with minor overreach or missing statistical detail. |
| 8-12 | Association, mechanism, or prediction is overstated; important statistics are absent. |
| 0-7 | Conclusion is unclear, unsupported, or far beyond the abstract evidence. |

## Novelty

| Points | Description |
|---:|---|
| 13-15 | New method, population, mechanism, model, intervention, or important new evidence. |
| 9-12 | Useful incremental evidence on a known question. |
| 5-8 | Routine replication or small extension. |
| 0-4 | Novelty is unclear from the abstract. |

## Caps

Apply caps after summing component scores:

| Condition | Maximum total |
|---|---:|
| No abstract | 30 |
| Abstract has background/objective only | 40 |
| No method details | 55 |
| No sample size or data source for human research | 70 |
| No primary outcome or main result | 75 |
| Study design cannot be identified | 80 |
| Case report or small case series | 70 |
| Narrative review or editorial/commentary | 65 |

Use `Need Full Text` when a paper may be important but a cap is triggered by missing abstract detail.
